Bael (Aegle marmelos)

Description: Bael, also known as Bilva, is a sacred tree in India. It is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine for its digestive, respiratory, and metabolic benefits.

Medicinal Uses

  • Supports digestive health and relieves constipation
  • Regulates blood sugar levels
  • Boosts immunity and fights infections
  • Improves respiratory health and reduces phlegm
  • Has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties
  • Strengthens the heart and promotes overall well-being

Habitat

Bael is native to India and Southeast Asia. It thrives in dry and arid regions, often found in forests and temple courtyards.

Part Used

Fruits, leaves, roots, and bark.

Where It Is Found in India

Bael is widely found in India, particularly in Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu. It grows in dry forests, temple gardens, and cultivated lands.

Preparation Methods

  • Consumed as fresh or dried fruit in herbal preparations
  • Boiled to make a detoxifying decoction
  • Used in Ayurvedic formulations for digestion
  • Applied as a paste for skin problems
  • Bael leaf juice is consumed for diabetes management
