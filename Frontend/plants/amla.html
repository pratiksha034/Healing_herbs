Amla (Phyllanthus emblica)

Description: Amla, also known as Indian Gooseberry, is a powerhouse of nutrients and is widely used in Ayurveda for its rejuvenating and immunity-boosting properties.

Medicinal Uses

  • Rich in Vitamin C and boosts immunity
  • Promotes healthy skin and hair growth
  • Aids digestion and improves gut health
  • Supports heart health and regulates cholesterol levels
  • Has antioxidant and anti-aging properties
  • Helps manage diabetes and blood sugar levels

Habitat

Amla is native to India and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Part Used

Fruits, seeds, and leaves.

Where It Is Found in India

Amla is widely found in India, particularly in Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu. It grows naturally in forests and is also cultivated for commercial purposes.

Preparation Methods

  • Consumed fresh or as juice for immunity
  • Used in Ayurvedic formulations like Chyawanprash
  • Dried and powdered for medicinal use
  • Infused in oils for hair and scalp nourishment
  • Boiled in water and consumed as a detox drink
