Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum)

Description: Tulsi, also known as Holy Basil, is a sacred plant in India and has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for thousands of years. It is revered for its medicinal properties and is often planted in homes and temples.

Medicinal Uses

  • Boosts immunity and fights infections
  • Relieves cold, cough, and respiratory disorders
  • Reduces stress, anxiety, and mental fatigue
  • Improves digestion and supports gut health
  • Regulates blood sugar levels and promotes heart health
  • Contains powerful antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties

Habitat

Tulsi is native to India and Southeast Asia, thriving in warm and tropical climates. It grows well in gardens and pots with well-drained soil.

Part Used

Leaves, roots, seeds, and flowers.

Where It Is Found in India

Tulsi is commonly found across India, especially in Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal, Madhya Pradesh, and Tamil Nadu. It is widely cultivated in home gardens, temples, and farms for medicinal and religious purposes.

Preparation Methods

  • Used in herbal tea to boost immunity
  • Extracted for essential oils with antimicrobial properties
  • Powdered and used in Ayurvedic medicines
  • Consumed raw or infused in water for detoxification
  • Applied as a paste for skin ailments
