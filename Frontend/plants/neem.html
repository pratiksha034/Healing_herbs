Neem (Azadirachta indica)

Description: Neem is a highly revered tree in Ayurveda, known for its powerful antibacterial, antifungal, and medicinal properties. It is widely used in herbal medicine and skincare.

Medicinal Uses

  • Boosts immunity and detoxifies the body
  • Promotes skin health and treats acne
  • Supports oral hygiene and prevents gum infections
  • Has antifungal and antibacterial properties
  • Helps regulate blood sugar levels
  • Used as an insect repellent and pesticide

Habitat

Neem is native to India and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

Part Used

Leaves, bark, seeds, and oil.

Where It Is Found in India

Neem is widely distributed across India, particularly in Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Maharashtra. It grows abundantly in villages, roadsides, and farmlands.

Preparation Methods

  • Used in herbal skincare products and soaps
  • Consumed as a tea for detoxification
  • Applied as a paste for skin conditions
  • Used in neem oil for hair and scalp health
  • Chewed raw for oral hygiene
